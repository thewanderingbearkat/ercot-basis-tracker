Congestion drivers — past year (SPP constraint prices × BETA shift factors, Jun’25–May’26)

Basis impact ($/MWh, modeled)
Computed the same way as the ERCOT and PJM congestion tools: authoritative hourly SPP binding-constraint shadow prices (ALL_CONSTRAINTS_PMV) weighted by our Yes Energy BETA shift factors, then differenced node − hub (Canadian Hills vs SPP South) so the result is the modeled contribution to basis. Impact = −(price × shift factor) averaged over all hours in the year; binding hours at right. Unlike McCrae — where one West-TX export GTC dominated — Canadian Hills congestion is diffuse: ~10 SPP 138–345 kV elements each contribute, no single fix. Ranking and binding-hours match nFront’s 4E-2 basis-to-hub table (Duncan, Davis–Mustang, Gracmont, Cleveland–AECI…); shift factors are modeled (BETA), so magnitudes are directional.

What moves congestion from here

Direction & rough magnitude of the forward drivers in nFront’s assumptions. The renewable build-out dwarfs the load growth, and the transmission relief is back-loaded to the 2030s — so the near-term push on Canadian Hills basis is negative.

Data flow — Canadian Hills (SPP)

SPP basis (node − SPP South) + the Anemoi 8760 generation + the curtailment record + Xweather flow into the hourly table; BETA shift factors drive the congestion attribution (cross-checked to nFront 4E). Results land in CM_CDNHILLS_* and feed the panels above. No SPP gen telemetry exists, so generation comes from the Anemoi file (not SCED) and shift factors are modeled BETA (not authoritative like ERCOT).
